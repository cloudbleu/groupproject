Dogville

Sweden

 

Filmed on a barren soundstage in Sweden, and set in Colorado, Dogville tells the story of Grace Mulligan, a woman who is hiding from mobsters. The people of the small town of Dogville, Colorado provide refuge for Grace in exchange for physical labor when she needs to hide from mobsters. Grace must win over the towns people in order to make the refuge "worth their while". We soon find out that not is all as it seems and the two-faced town increase their demands on Grace, and start to resent her helpless outsider position. Directed by Lars Von Trier, starring Nicole Kidman, Chloë Sevigny, Paul Bettany, Stellan Skarsgård.

 
 

5* Empire Online Review

"Bold stylistic decisions reinforce a narrative that's underpinned by convincing performances."

Code Girls Review

The Dogme 95 Manifesto started by the Danish directors Lars Von Trier and Thomas Vinterberg set about making film an art form again. The manifesto stated several rules for filmmaking, one of which included not using special effects or technology. These rules can be seen in Lars Von Trier's Dogville. Filmed on a minimalist set, with no walls between the houses on set, it can be argued that Dogville is a work of out. The film is full of unexpected twists and turns, and it will keep the viewers attention until the climatic final scene.

 
 
 
 
 
 
 

Sweet Sixteen

Scotland

 

Set in Greenock, Port Glasgow in Scotland, Sweet Sixteen tells the story of Scottish teenage boy Liam before he turns sixteen. With his mother on the cusp of being released for a crime that she did not commit, Liam sets about trying to make a fresh start for them. While trying to evade his grandfather and stepfather who used his mother as a drug mule, Liam attempts to acquire by stealing and selling his grandfather's drugs on the street. Directed by Ken Loach, starring Martin Compston (Line of Duty).

 
 

4* by the Guardian

"The hero of Ken Loach's new film is Liam, a lairy young lad a few weeks shy of his 16th birthday, caught with his friend Pinball selling contraband fags in a pub, without reference to HM customs and excise. Chased out by the publican, they give him - and us, and anyone who wants to know - the finger, American-style. What a long time ago it seems that David Bradley's Billy was on the Kes posters and paperback covers, flicking us a good old-fashioned British V-sign."

Code Girls Review

Sweet Sixteen shows the struggle of a teenage boy called Liam and his fight to set up a normal life for his mother who is about to be released from prison. Directed by Ken Loach, a socialist concerned with social issues such as homelessness, Sweet Sixteen shows the struggles that people in Glasgow face during after the Thatcher government closed some of the port trade in Scotland. The film can take you on a roller coaster, one minute you are laughing at the antics and Liam and his friends get up to, until reality kicks in and Liam makes a rash decision. This film can really make you think about the problems that some people are unfortunately born into.

 
 
 
 
 
 
 

A Bout de Souffle

France

 

A bout de Souffle was set and filmed in France. When a small time criminal Michel accidentally kills a policeman he turns to his journalist girlfriend Patricia for help. The once "sauve" bad guy who thinks of himself as Humphrey Bogart must now hide in Paris before he can get together enough money to flee to Italy. Although he wants to take Patricia with him, she soon has doubts after learning that Michel is on the run from a murder charge. Directed by Jean-Luc Godard.

 
 

The Guardian by Derek Malcolm

"A Bout de Souffle (Breathless). It reached us like a clap of thunder in 1960, immediately establishing an international reputation for itself and introducing us to a new kind of paradoxical hero who has been copied, usually badly, ever since. "Squealers squeal, burglars burgle, killers kill, lovers love," he says to his American girlfriend. In other words, what we are determines what we do."

Code Girls Review

One of the first and most influential examples of French New Wave (nouvelle vague) cinema, A Bout de Souffle showcases France's new film direction.
